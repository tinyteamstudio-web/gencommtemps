Getting Started

Step 1: Start with What You Know

You don’t need complete information to begin. Names, basic relationships, and partial details are enough.

Step 2: Build Real Relationships

TinyTeam supports real-life structures — blended families, multiple parents, extended relatives, and evolving communities.

Step 3: Honor Loved Ones

You may mark loved ones who have passed away using an In Loving Memory designation. Their place remains visible and respected.

Step 4: Grow Over Time

Your library is not meant to be finished in one sitting. You can return anytime to add more people, stories, or updates.

Step 5: Decide on Output Later

Your library can remain cloud-based or be prepared later for printing (A5, A4, A3, or book formats). You don’t need to decide now.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if our family or community setup is complicated?

TinyTeam supports real-life situations including blended families, multiple parents, adopted members, and extended relationships.

Can we start small and expand later?

Yes. Many users begin with a small group and grow their library as more information becomes available.

How are deceased loved ones handled?

You can mark a person as In Loving Memory. Their role remains part of the family or community record.

Is printing handled automatically?

Yes. Layout, margins, and binding rules are handled automatically when you choose a print format.

Do we own our data?

Yes. Your data belongs to you and remains part of your library as it grows over time.
