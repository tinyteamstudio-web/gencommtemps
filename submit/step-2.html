Step 2 · People & Relationships

This step helps us understand who is part of your family or group and how people are related to each other.

Important reminder

You do not need to list everyone at once. You may start with just a few people.

It is okay if dates, spellings, or details are incomplete. This can be updated later.

How many people are included? (estimate)

You may simply choose one:

  • 1–10 people
  • 11–30 people
  • 31–100 people
  • More than 100
  • Not sure yet

Prepare a simple list of people

For each person, please prepare the following information:

  • Full name
  • Relationship
    (example: father, mother, child, spouse, sibling, member)
  • Status
    Living · Deceased · Prefer not to say

Dates of birth or death are not required at this stage.

Example (very simple)

You may write your list like this: 

Juan Dela Cruz – Father – Deceased
Maria Dela Cruz – Mother – Living
Ana Dela Cruz – Daughter – Living

This does not need to be perfect. It only helps us understand the structure.

If relationships feel complicated

You may describe relationships in your own words, such as:

  • “First marriage”
  • “Second partner”
  • “Adopted child”
  • “Raised by grandparents”

You do not need to explain everything here. Detailed or sensitive notes will be handled in the next step.
