Step 3 · Special Situations & Private Notes

This step is for anything that needs extra care — blended families, adoption, sensitive histories, privacy requests, or “In Loving Memory” notes.

Important reminder

You only share what you are comfortable sharing. If something is sensitive, you may keep it simple or say “private”.

This information will be handled respectfully and used only to structure your digital library correctly.

Check any that apply (you may choose many)

  • Blended family (multiple marriages or partners)
  • Same father or mother, different partners
  • Adoption (formal or informal)
  • Unknown or undisclosed parent
  • Raised by grandparents or relatives
  • Sensitive family history (keep details private)
  • Large extended family (many branches)
  • Community with sub-groups or units
  • Multiple people with the same name

Write your private notes (simple is okay)

You may write notes in your own words. Examples: 

• Two marriages for (Name). Children from both marriages.
• One adopted child (keep details private).
• Unknown father for one branch.
• Please keep one person’s details minimal (privacy).
• Two cousins have the same name; add nickname labels.

If you prefer, you can write: “Explain privately by email” and we will ask gently.

In Loving Memory (Deceased Loved Ones)

If you would like memorial pages, you may note:

  • Name
  • Short message or tribute (optional)
  • Photos you want included (optional)

Memorial pages are handled with respect and care. Nothing is shared publicly unless you request it.

Privacy requests (optional)

If you want to keep certain details private, you may request:

  • Use initials instead of full names
  • Hide dates or sensitive details
  • Keep certain pages minimal
  • Do not include specific photos
